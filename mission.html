Vision, Mission & Our Story

Why we built this, what we're trying to change, and the three people behind every feature.

🔭
Our Vision

A world where every Gen Z Indian is financially literate before they turn 20 — not because a textbook forced them to be, but because a tool made it actually make sense.

We want to be the generation that broke the cycle of "hum log toh bas naukri karte hain, paisa manage karna toh baad mein dekhenge." The generation that started investing at 18, hit their first crore at 35, and understood exactly what was happening at every step.

🎯
Our Mission

Build financial tools that actually speak Gen Z — in Hinglish, with honesty, with humour, and with real actionable advice instead of vague platitudes.

1
Demystify money — break down SIPs, budgeting, compounding and goals in language a 19-year-old in a hostel room actually understands
2
Create action, not just awareness — give specific, personalised recommendations based on your actual income, city, and goals
3
Build habits before bad ones form — the spending patterns you set at 20 follow you to 35. We intervene early, with equal parts honesty and sarcasm
4
Make wealth-building feel possible — not a privilege for the already-wealthy, but a realistic path for anyone starting with ₹500 and consistency
"Financial literacy shouldn't feel scary — it should feel empowering. We built Roast&Rich because every young person in India deserves a financial co-pilot that's honest, smart, and doesn't talk down to them."
The people behind it
Meet the Team 👋

Built at IGDTUW for Protex Hackathon 2026 · Three people, one shared belief: Gen Z deserves better financial tools.

Founder
V
Vaishnavi
Founder · Roast&Rich · IGDTUW · Protex Hackathon 2026
LinkedIn

I grew up in a home where finance was never a mystery. With my father coming from a finance background, I often heard things like “compounding is the eighth wonder of the world” long before I truly understood them. His fascination with numbers and his small saving lessons shaped my mindset early on.

By nature, I’m minimalistic — I like saving and keeping things simple. Still, I often found myself unsure. I didn’t always know where my money was going or what habits were affecting my savings. Knowing the theory is one thing; handling it alone feels very different.

Building this app changed that. What began as a basic hackathon project became something I genuinely cared about. The more I worked on it, the more invested I became — and it made me feel productive and proud. I learned things my science background never taught: financial terms, spending patterns, and the importance of tracking even the smallest habits.

And I didn’t build it alone. Every feature and every “this would actually help” moment came from conversations with my teammates. Together, we shaped something real and useful — from the mood tracker to the budget planner to the FOMO checker.

Now, I’m excited to see how this app helps others the way it helped me. I want us to feel more aware, more confident, and less confused about their money. And honestly, I can’t wait to hear what people think when they finally try it.

💡
What problem are we actually solving?

India has a massive financial literacy gap — especially for young people. Most personal finance content is too old-school, too complicated, too generic, or trying to sell you something. Roast&Rich is none of those. No ads. No commissions. No agenda except helping you understand your money and build better habits — at your pace, in your language.
