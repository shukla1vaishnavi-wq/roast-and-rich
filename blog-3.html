What's your FIRE number? The formula that tells you when you're finally free

📅 2026⏱ 6 min read🔥 Must read

Imagine waking up one day and realizing you never have to work again. Not because you're sick. Not because you're old. But because your investments are generating enough money to cover your entire life — forever.

That's FIRE. And there's a number you need to hit to get there.

What is FIRE?

FIRE = Financial Independence, Retire Early. It's a movement (originally from the West, now catching on in India) where people aggressively save and invest early so they can stop working way before the traditional retirement age of 60.

We're talking 35. 40. Some legends even hit it at 32.

🧮 The FIRE formula: Your FIRE number = Annual Expenses × 25

Let's do the actual math

Say you currently spend ₹40,000/month = ₹4,80,000/year.

Your FIRE number:

₹4,80,000 × 25 = ₹1.2 Crore

Once your invested corpus hits ₹1.2 Crore and is growing at ~12%/year, you can theoretically withdraw 4% annually (the "4% rule") and never run out of money. That's ₹4.8 Lakh/year = ₹40,000/month. Forever.

Why "× 25"?

Because 1 ÷ 25 = 4%. The 4% rule says if you withdraw 4% of your corpus annually, a diversified investment portfolio historically never runs out — because it keeps growing faster than your withdrawals.

⚠️ India-specific caveat: Inflation in India runs higher (~6-7% vs 2-3% in the US). So for India, many FIRE planners suggest using 3% withdrawal (×33 multiplier) to be safer. Your FIRE number might be Annual Expenses × 30-33 for a more conservative approach.

Types of FIRE

🍃 Lean FIRE

Retire with the minimum — a simple, frugal life. Spend ₹20-25k/month, need corpus of ₹60-75 Lakh. Hard but achievable young.

⚖️ Regular FIRE

Comfortable middle-class life. Spend ₹50k/month, need ₹1.5 Crore. Target for most people.

💎 Fat FIRE

Luxury retirement. Spend ₹2L+/month, need ₹6+ Crore. Takes longer but some people aim here.

How Gen Z can actually hit FIRE

  1. Calculate your FIRE number now — even a rough estimate focuses your mind
  2. Track your expenses — use the Daily Tracker here on Roast&Rich
  3. Invest 30-40% of income from Day 1 — the earlier, the smaller the sacrifice
  4. Increase income, not just cut expenses — skills, freelance, side hustles
  5. Use equity SIPs for the long-term corpus — FDs won't get you to FIRE

FIRE isn't about never working again if you love your work. It's about having the choice. Working because you want to, not because you have to. That choice is worth building towards — starting today.
